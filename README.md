 Equity Research & Portfolio Allocation Project

## Overview
This project analyzes the financial performance of major technology companies—Apple, Microsoft, and Nvidia—compared to the S&P 500 index. The goal is to evaluate the company's performance using key financial metrics and to develop a data-driven investment strategy.

## Objective
- Compare the financial and market performance of selected companies
- Identify trends in revenue growth, profitability, and stock returns
- Evaluate risk vs. return characteristics
- Develop a portfolio allocation strategy based on findings

## Companies Analyzed
- Apple
- Microsoft
- Nvidia

## Benchmark
- S&P 500 Index

## Tools & Technologies
- Microsoft Excel (data analysis, financial modeling)
- Python (data cleaning and analysis)
- Tableau (data visualization and dashboards)

## Key Metrics Used
- Revenue Growth
- Net Income & Profit Margins
- Earnings Per Share (EPS)
- Stock Price Performance
- Volatility and Risk Measures

## Analysis
The project involves collecting historical financial and stock data, cleaning and organizing datasets, and performing comparative analysis across companies. Visualizations are created to highlight trends and differences in performance.

## Results & Insights
- Identified differences in growth rates and profitability across companies
- Evaluated stock performance relative to the S&P 500
- Assessed trade-offs between growth potential and risk

## Portfolio Allocation Strategy
Based on the analysis, a sample portfolio allocation was developed:

- Microsoft – 40%
- Apple – 35%
- Nvidia – 25%

This allocation balances stability and growth by emphasizing consistent performers while maintaining exposure to high-growth opportunities.

## Conclusion
This project demonstrates the application of financial analysis and data analytics to real-world investment decision-making. The findings highlight how different companies offer varying risk-return profiles and how data-driven strategies can be used to optimize portfolio allocation.
