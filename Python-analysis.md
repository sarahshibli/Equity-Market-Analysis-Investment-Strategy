## Python Market Analysis 
### Overview
This section analyzes stock performance for Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, and the S&P 500 using Python. The analysis focuses on return, risk, and correlation to evaluate investment performance and portfolio behavior.

---

### Data Collection

- Historical stock data was retrieved using the 'yfinance' library
- Daily closing prices were used to calculate returns
- Data covers the period from 2022 to 2025

---

### Annualized Returns

- Nvidia delivers significantly higher returns (~65%) compared to all other assets
- Apple and Microsoft generate moderate returns (~12-15%)
- The S&P 500 provides a lower benchmark return (~8%)

**This indicates Nvidia is in a high-growth phase, while Apple and Microsoft provide more stable performance.**

---

### Volatility (Risk)

- Nvidia exhibits the highest volatility (~55%), indicating large price fluctuations
- Apple and Microsoft show moderate volatility (~27%)
- The S&P 500 has the lowest volatility (~17%), reflecting greater stability

**This highlights the trade-off between risk and return, where higher returns are associated with greater uncertainty.**

---

### Insights

- Nvidia provides the highest return but also carries significantly higher risk
- Apple and Microsoft offer more balanced risk-return profiles
- Strong correlation across assets reduces the effectiveness of diversification within the same sector
- The S&P 500 serves as a stable benchmark for comparison

---

### Conclusion
The analysis demonstrates a clear risk-return trade-off. Nividia offers high growth potential but comes with elevated volatility, making it suitable for aggressive investment strategies. Apple and Microsoft provide more stable and consistent performance, making them better suited for balanced portfolios. Despite Nvidia’s strong returns, its high correlation with the broader market limits diversification benefits.
